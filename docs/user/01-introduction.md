# DidHub: Your guide to digital identity management

DidHub is a friendly platform that helps people manage Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs) and the data that goes with them. At the heart of DidHub is DIDAlterHub, the platform that powers your everyday work with DIDs. It's designed for individuals and teams who want a clear, reliable way to handle digital identities without getting lost in technical details.

What is DIDAlterHub?
- DIDAlterHub is the platform for managing DID-related data and workflows. In plain terms, it's the place where your DIDs and the information attached to them live. It connects the pieces you need—the identifiers, the data that describes them, and the tasks you perform—so you can focus on your goals rather than on the underlying tech.

Key features at a glance
- Central hub for all your DIDs and their data
- Simple workflows that guide you through common tasks
- A shared space for teams to collaborate securely
- Clear dashboards that show progress and status at a glance
- Safe storage with built-in privacy and access controls
- Notifications and reminders to keep things moving

How you interact with DidHub
- You see a list of DIDs you manage, each with a card showing key details
- You can add new DIDs or update information without needing to understand the underlying systems
- Workflows walk you through steps, from setup to ongoing maintenance
- You can organize DIDs into projects or collections to keep related items together
- You control who can view or edit items, making collaboration easy and secure
- The app provides simple search and filters to find the right DID quickly

Why people use DidHub
- Keep all your DID data in one safe place, so nothing falls through the cracks
- Move from idea to action with guided workflows, not guesswork
- Work with teammates without complicated handoffs or emails
- See progress at a glance, so you know what’s done and what’s left
- Rely on clear, human-friendly language rather than technical jargon

Getting started (a quick overview)
- Sign in to your DidHub account
- Create your first DID and attach basic information
- Pick a workflow to begin a common task
- Invite teammates and set who can see what

If you would like a deeper dive later, we will cover what to expect in developer and technical docs, but this introduction is designed to be friendly for everyone.
